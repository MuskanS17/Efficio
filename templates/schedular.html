OS Scheduling

Scheduling Algorithms
  1. First-Come-First-Served (FCFS)
    Executes in arrival order. Disadvantage: Can cause a "convoy effect."
  2. Shortest Job First (SJF)
    Executes the shortest burst time first. Disadvantage: May cause starvation.
  3. Round Robin (RR)
    Each process gets a fixed time slice. Disadvantage: Improper quantum affects efficiency.
  4. Shortest Remaining Time First (SRTF)
    Preemptive version of SJF. Disadvantage: Longer processes may starve.
  5. Highest Response Ratio Next (HRRN)
    Prioritizes processes with high response ratio. Disadvantage: Adds computational overhead.

Page Fault

Page Replacement Algorithms
  1. First-In-First-Out (FIFO)
    Replaces the oldest page in memory. Disadvantage: Suffers from Belady's anomaly.
  2. Least Recently Used (LRU)
    Replaces the least recently used page. Disadvantage: Requires extra tracking overhead.
  3. Optimal Page Replacement
    Replaces the page that won't be used for the longest time. Disadvantage: Requires future knowledge.
